Music Skillet

Graph-based recommendation web app for Outside Lands festival suggestions. Simply log in to your Spotify account and let the app retrieve your list of starred tracks, and from there, choose one of four available criteria to base your recommendations on. From there, the app will use Gracenote, Echonest, and Keen.io API data to suggest you performances to watch during the weekend of the Outside Lands music festival. Erase your feelings of indecisive music choices now!

This project was built at Outside Hacks, a 24-hour hackathon that challenges developers and designers to hack innovative apps to improve music artists' and fans' experience at the Outside Lands music festival. We won a prize for Best Use of the Spotify/Echonest APIs.

View press coverage here.

  • Date: July 2014
  • Technologies Used: HTML/CSS, JavaScript, Spotify/Echonest APIs
  • Collaborators: Shaohan Chen

NextWatch

Working with 2 other teammates for 24 hours at the TV of Tomorrow Show (TVOT) hackathon in San Francisco, I built an iOS TV recommendation app that uses automated content recognition (ACR) via Gracenote's Entourage API to allow one's iPhone/iPad to detect what he/she watching on TV and retrieve metadata (i.e. genre, cast, crew, etc) of the TV show/movie. From there, the app uses this information to build a TV watcher profile, including statistics of previously watched shows, and uses this profile to recommend future shows to watch at any given time.

Our app received a special honorable mention from the director/host of the hackathon for being likely to have high future market potential.

View press coverages here and here.

  • Date: June 2014
  • Technologies Used: iOS, Objective C, Gracenote Entourage API
  • Collaborators: Shaohan Chen, Ray Kung

Hack Buddies

Working with one other Carnegie Mellon student, I designed a web app (using Python, Django, PHP, SQL, SQLite, HTML, CSS, and JavaScript) in a 36-hour timeframe that allows hackathon participants to quickly search for the most compatible teammates to work with based on their student hacker profile and whatever criteria they care about and select (i.e., programming skill set, type of app interested in, project interests/ideas, etc).
